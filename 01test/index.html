Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the responsible, ethical, and safe use of digital technologies, such as the internet, social media, devices, and online platforms.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law (also called cyber law) refers to the rules and regulations that govern how technology, the internet, and digital information are used.

What is AUP?

An Acceptable Use Policy is a set of rules that explains how technology, networks, and digital resources are allowed to be used within an organization such as a school, workplace, or company.

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is using someone else's work, ideas, or words without giving proper credit and presenting them as your own.

It doesn't matter whether it's done on purpose or by accident-it's still plagiarism

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic Dishonesty is any action that gives a student an unfair academic advantage or misrepresents their own work in an educational setting.

In simple terms: it's cheating or being dishonest in schoolwork

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

AI-generated content (text, images, code, audio, video) may be used only when allowed and only in responsible ways. AI is a tool to support learning-not a replacement for thinking or original work.
