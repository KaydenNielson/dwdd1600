Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress, famous for his large, bold signature.
Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence; later became the 3rd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Renowned statesman, inventor, and diplomat.
John Adams
Key advocate for independence; later became the 2nd President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
Political leader and organizer of resistance against British rule in Massachusetts.
Roger Sherman
Statesman from Connecticut who helped draft and sign the Declaration.
Robert R. Livingston
Member of the Committee of Five that helped draft the Declaration of Independence.
Charles Carroll
Signer from Maryland and the only Catholic to sign the Declaration.

Each of these individuals played a significant role in the founding of the United States and is remembered for their courage in signing the Declaration, an act considered treasonous by the British Crown.
